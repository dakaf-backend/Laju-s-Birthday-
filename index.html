A Word (Okay, Several) About You

Laju, my love — today the whole calendar exists just to make room for you. One day was never going to be enough to cover everything glorious about you, but the Gregorian calendar has its limits, so we'll do our best with the 24 hours we've got.

You are, objectively, the most extraordinary person I know. Funny without trying, sharp without being cruel about it, and somehow still patient with me on the days I absolutely do not deserve it. You walk into a room and it rearranges itself around you. This is not an exaggeration. I have watched furniture consider it.

"She's not just beautiful — she's got a five-lane forehead with its own postcode." — an actual thought I had, unprompted, while making breakfast.

And yes, we have to talk about the forehead. NASA has reportedly inquired about using it as a backup satellite dish. When the sun hits it just right, ships at sea have reported a second sunrise. It's not a forehead, Laju, it's a whole event space — and honestly? It suits you. There's more room up there for all the brilliant thoughts you keep having, and someone has to house them.

Jokes aside — genuinely, truly aside — you make ordinary days feel like they were worth showing up for. You are kind in a way that costs you something and you do it anyway. You are glorious not because I'm biased (I am, extremely) but because it's just accurate reporting. Happy birthday, forehead and all. Especially the forehead.
